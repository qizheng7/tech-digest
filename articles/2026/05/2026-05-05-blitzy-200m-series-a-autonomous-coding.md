## Summary

Blitzy, a Cambridge-based AI startup that autonomously completes large-scale software development projects for enterprises, raised a $200M Series A led by Northzone at a $1.4B valuation. The platform orchestrates thousands of AI agents in parallel to understand and modify legacy codebases, with built-in testing and validation targeting 5x engineering velocity. The round includes strategic investments from Liberty Mutual, Erie Strategic Ventures, and BAL Ventures, signaling strong traction in regulated industries.

## Key Highlights

- $200M Series A led by Northzone, with Battery Ventures, PSG, Jump Capital, Morgan Creek Digital, and Defiant; $1.4B valuation
- Claims 66.5% score on SWE-Bench Pro — record-breaking at time of announcement, above other major competitors
- 5x engineering velocity for enterprise customers on large-scale codebase projects
- Orchestrates thousands of AI agents simultaneously for autonomous testing and validation
- Founded by serial entrepreneur Brian Elliott (former Army Ranger) and Sid Pardeshi (NVIDIA Master Inventor, 27+ neural network patents)
- Expanding into government, financial services, and insurance sectors with new funding

## Why It Matters

Blitzy represents the accelerating shift from AI as a developer assistant to AI as an autonomous software contractor — directly targeting the multi-trillion-dollar legacy codebase modernization market in regulated enterprises.
