## Summary

AI chipmaker Cerebras Systems filed for a $3.5B IPO on Nasdaq, targeting a valuation of up to $26.6B by selling 28 million shares at $115–$125 each. The company reported Q4 2025 revenue of $510M (76% YoY growth) with $87.9M net income, and is best known for its wafer-scale chip architecture that it positions as a high-speed alternative to NVIDIA's GPU clusters. A landmark deal with OpenAI—750MW of AI compute through 2028 worth $20B+—anchors its revenue pipeline.

## Key Highlights

- IPO target: $3.5B raise on Nasdaq; shares priced at $115–$125 (28M shares + 4.2M greenshoe)
- Valuation: up to $26.6B, up from $23B valuation in February 2026 round (AMD invested)
- Q4 2025 revenue: $510M (+76% YoY), net income: $87.9M
- OpenAI deal: 750MW of AI computing capacity through 2028, $20B+ transaction value
- CEO Andrew Feldman retaining all shares post-IPO (owns 10.3M shares worth up to $1.28B)
- Multiple banks are co-underwriting; mid-May offering targeted

## Why It Matters

Cerebras going public is the biggest test yet of whether NVIDIA alternatives can attract durable public market capital, and their locked-in OpenAI partnership provides the kind of revenue visibility that could win over institutional investors.
