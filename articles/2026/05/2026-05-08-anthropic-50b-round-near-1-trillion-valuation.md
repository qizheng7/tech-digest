## Summary

The Financial Times reported that Anthropic is in early discussions to raise as much as $50 billion at a valuation approaching $900 billion–$1 trillion, which would make it the most valuable private AI company in the world — surpassing OpenAI's $852 billion valuation set in March 2026. The round is driven by explosive revenue growth: Anthropic's annualized revenue is approaching $45 billion, a fivefold increase from $9 billion at end of 2024. Interested investors include Dragoneer, General Catalyst, and Lightspeed Venture Partners, with a board decision potentially as early as May 2026.

## Key Highlights

- Anthropic exploring $50B raise at ~$900B–$1T valuation, up from $380B in its February Series G
- Annualized revenue approaching $45B — fivefold growth from $9B at end of 2024, driven largely by Claude Code adoption
- Secondary market trades on Forge Global already implying $1T+ valuation, driven by supply-demand imbalance in available shares
- If closed at these terms, Anthropic would surpass OpenAI ($852B) as the most valuable private AI company
- IPO targeting as early as October 2026, advised by Goldman Sachs and JPMorgan at a $400–500B target (pre-funding discussions)
- Dragoneer, General Catalyst, and Lightspeed among interested lead investors

## Why It Matters

Anthropic's revenue trajectory — reaching near-OpenAI revenue levels with far fewer products — signals that the enterprise Claude market and developer API are scaling faster than most expected, validating the "AI as a service" model at a company still burning significant compute costs.
