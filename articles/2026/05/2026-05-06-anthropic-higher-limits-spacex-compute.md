## Summary

Anthropic announced three immediate service improvements alongside a major infrastructure partnership with SpaceX. Usage limits for Claude Code were doubled across all plans, peak-hours reductions were eliminated for Pro and Max users, and API rate limits for Opus models were significantly increased. The infrastructure news is larger: Anthropic secured access to SpaceX's Colossus 1 data center, gaining over 300 megawatts of new capacity (220,000+ NVIDIA GPUs) available within a month.

## Key Highlights

- Claude Code's 5-hour rate limits doubled on Pro, Max, Team, and Enterprise plans; peak-hour restrictions removed for Pro/Max
- API rate limits for Claude Opus models significantly increased
- SpaceX Colossus 1 deal adds >300 MW / 220,000+ NVIDIA GPUs within ~1 month
- Joins existing compute deals: Amazon (up to 5 GW), Google (5 GW with Broadcom), Microsoft/NVIDIA ($30B Azure), and a $50B Fluidstack investment
- Anthropic signaled interest in "multiple gigawatts of orbital AI compute capacity" with SpaceX long-term
- Committed to expanding international infrastructure to serve regulated industries requiring regional data residency

## Why It Matters

Compute access has been the bottleneck limiting Claude's availability to paying customers; this multi-partner infrastructure diversification strategy is Anthropic's direct answer to closing the capacity gap with OpenAI and Google at enterprise scale.
