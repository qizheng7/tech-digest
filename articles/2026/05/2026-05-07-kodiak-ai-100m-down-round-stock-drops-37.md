## Summary

Kodiak AI (formerly Kodiak Robotics), the autonomous trucking startup that went public via SPAC in September 2025, raised $100 million in a deeply discounted offering — pricing shares at $6.50 versus a $9.10 market close — triggering a 37% after-hours stock drop. The raise comes despite deteriorating financials: Q1 2026 revenue was just $1.8 million against an operating loss of $37.8 million, double the prior year's losses. The company is burning cash faster than it's growing, and the discounted raise signals limited access to capital at fair-market terms.

## Key Highlights

- Raised $100M at $6.50/share, a 29% discount to the $9.10 closing price; stock fell 37% in after-hours trading
- Q1 2026 revenue: $1.8M vs. $37.8M operating loss — cash burn doubled year-over-year
- Investors: existing backer Ares Management plus unnamed institutional investors
- Business: owns trucks, provides safety drivers, hauls freight for Werner, J.B. Hunt, and Roehl Transport
- Plans to transition to driverless "driver-as-a-service" model later in 2026 once autonomous ops launch
- Original SPAC valuation: ~$2.5B in September 2025; implied valuation now substantially lower

## Why It Matters

Kodiak's down round illustrates the harsh reality for physical-AI startups bridging the gap from demo to revenue: even well-funded autonomous trucking companies face a brutal path to unit economics, especially when public markets demand profitability progress alongside capability claims.
