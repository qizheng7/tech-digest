## Summary

Sierra, Bret Taylor and Clay Bavor's enterprise AI agent startup, raised a $950M Series E at a $15.8B post-money valuation led by Tiger Global and Google's GV. The company reached $150M ARR in just 8 quarters—a milestone no traditional software company has reportedly hit so quickly—and now counts over 40% of the Fortune 50 as clients including Prudential, Cigna, Blue Cross Blue Shield, and Rocket Mortgage. The round follows a $10B valuation from fall 2025 and reflects accelerating enterprise demand for AI agent platforms.

## Key Highlights

- $950M Series E at $15.8B valuation (up from $10B in fall 2025); led by Tiger Global and GV
- Additional investors: Benchmark, Sequoia, Greenoaks
- $150M ARR in 8 quarters—fastest ever claimed for traditional software ARR growth
- >40% Fortune 50 penetration; clients include Prudential, Cigna, BCBS, Rocket Mortgage
- Co-founders Bret Taylor (OpenAI board chair) and Clay Bavor became billionaires in September 2025
- AINews noted they crossed $100M ARR in November 2025 and $150M ARR in February 2026

## Why It Matters

Sierra's trajectory validates the enterprise AI agent category as a standalone market—not just an add-on to existing SaaS—and the Tiger-led round signals that growth-stage investors are willing to pay frontier multiples for proven agent-first platforms.
