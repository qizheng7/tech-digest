## Summary

Cerebras Systems raised its IPO price range from $115–$125 to $150–$160 per share after the offering attracted orders for more than 20 times the available shares, reflecting surging investor demand ahead of the May 13 pricing date. At the top of the new range, Cerebras would raise approximately $4.8 billion — up from the originally planned $3.5 billion — making it the largest global IPO of 2026 to date. The offering is listed on Nasdaq under the ticker "CBRS" and is led by Morgan Stanley, Citigroup, Barclays, and UBS.

## Key Highlights

- New price range: $150–$160/share (up from $115–$125), with shares marketed increasing to 30M from 28M
- Raise target: ~$4.8B at top of range, up from $3.5B under original terms (37% increase)
- Demand: 20x oversubscribed; pricing date set for May 13, 2026
- Ticker: CBRS on Nasdaq Global Select Market; led by Morgan Stanley, Citigroup, Barclays, UBS
- Largest IPO globally in 2026 to date, per Dealogic
- Cerebras competes with NVIDIA on AI chip performance using its wafer-scale engine architecture

## Why It Matters

The 20x oversubscription signals that public market appetite for AI infrastructure hardware has dramatically rebounded, and Cerebras's pricing surge validates the market's conviction that NVIDIA alternatives can command significant premiums.
