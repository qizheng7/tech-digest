## Summary

Genesis AI unveiled GENE-26.5, its first robotics foundation model, alongside human-proportioned robotic hands and a sensor-loaded data collection glove. The demo showed the system performing complex dexterous tasks including cooking, piano playing, and Rubik's cube solving. The company's full-stack approach combines proprietary AI models, custom hardware (built with Wuji Tech), and simulation infrastructure to close the "embodiment gap" that limits other robotics AI approaches.

## Key Highlights

- GENE-26.5 trained across language, vision, proprioception, tactile, and action modalities simultaneously
- Demo showcased cooking, smoothie preparation, piano playing, and Rubik's cube solving
- $105M seed round co-led by Eclipse and Khosla Ventures (July 2025); investors include Eric Schmidt and Vladlen Koltun
- Robotic hands match human hand proportions (vs. standard two-finger grippers) enabling richer behavioral data collection
- Data collection glove mimics human hand anatomy to address the embodiment gap in training data
- Competes with Physical Intelligence, Skild AI, and other physical-AI startups

## Why It Matters

Full-stack robotics companies that control both AI and hardware are gaining a data flywheel advantage over software-only players, as dexterous manipulation tasks require tight integration of sensing, actuation, and multimodal AI.
