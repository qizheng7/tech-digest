## Summary

SAP agreed to acquire Prior Labs, an 18-month-old German AI startup, with a commitment to invest €1 billion (~$1.16B) over four years, plus a reported "well over half a billion dollars" in upfront cash to founders. Prior Labs builds tabular foundation models (TFMs) that work with structured enterprise data — tables, databases, spreadsheets — rather than unstructured text, with its TabPFN model series downloaded over 3 million times in open-source form. SAP's CTO framed the deal around structured data as "the greatest untapped opportunity in enterprise AI."

## Key Highlights

- SAP paying ~€1B over four years plus substantial upfront cash for a startup that previously raised only $9.3M in pre-seed (Feb 2025)
- Prior Labs' TabPFN is a tabular foundation model designed for the structured data formats where enterprise information actually lives
- TabPFN series has 3M+ open-source downloads, signaling genuine developer traction before the acquisition
- SAP simultaneously announced it will authorize NVIDIA's NemoClaw agentic framework while blocking OpenClaw — a selective AI governance stance
- Previous investor: Balderton Capital

## Why It Matters

SAP's bet signals that enterprise AI's next battleground is structured data, not text, as most enterprise value lives in databases and spreadsheets rather than documents — and specialized foundation models for tabular data could be a durable moat.
