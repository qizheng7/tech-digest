## Summary

Google DeepMind published an update on AlphaEvolve, a Gemini-powered coding agent that autonomously discovers and optimizes algorithms across scientific, engineering, and commercial domains. The system has already been deployed across Google's internal infrastructure — from TPU circuit design to Google Spanner write amplification reduction — and is now available to external partners through Google Cloud, achieving measurable results including a 30% reduction in genomic sequencing errors and doubling transformer training speed for Klarna.

## Key Highlights

- Grid optimization: increased feasible AC Optimal Power Flow solutions from 14% to 88%
- Quantum circuits: 10x lower error rates for Google's Willow quantum processor
- Google infrastructure: 20% reduction in write amplification in Spanner; improved cache replacement policies
- Mathematics: solved Erdős problems; improved bounds on Traveling Salesman and Ramsey Numbers
- Genomics: 30% reduction in DNA sequencing variant detection errors via DeepConsensus improvement
- Commercial results: Klarna doubled transformer training speed; FM Logistic achieved 10.4% routing improvement; Schrödinger got 4x speedup in ML force field training

## Why It Matters

AlphaEvolve demonstrates AI's capacity for autonomous algorithm discovery at production scale, moving beyond code generation to actively improving the infrastructure and science that AI systems themselves depend on.
