## Summary

xAI released Grok 4.3 on May 6, 2026, with a focus on agentic tool calling and instruction following, alongside aggressive pricing reductions of ~38% on input and ~58% on output tokens compared to Grok 4.20. The model features a 1-million-token context window with reasoning support, function calling, structured outputs, and prompt caching. It scores 53 on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index, placing just above Claude Sonnet 4.6 but roughly 276 points behind GPT-5.5.

## Key Highlights

- Priced at $1.25/M input and $2.50/M output — ~40% cheaper input, ~60% cheaper output vs. Grok 4.20
- 321-point increase on GDPval-AA agentic benchmarks (1179 → 1500 ELO); leads on instruction-following and enterprise task benchmarks
- 1M token context window with reasoning, function calling, structured outputs, and prompt caching
- 81% score on IFBench (instruction following); 98% on telecom-specific benchmarks
- Accuracy improved 8 points on omniscience tests; hallucination rate degraded by ~8 points vs. predecessor
- Available via xAI API, Vercel AI Gateway, and partner platforms as `grok-4.3` or `grok-4.3-latest`

## Why It Matters

xAI is using aggressive price cuts and agentic specialization to carve out enterprise share against OpenAI and Anthropic, making Grok 4.3 a credible cost-efficient alternative for tool-heavy agent workloads even as it trails on raw intelligence benchmarks.
