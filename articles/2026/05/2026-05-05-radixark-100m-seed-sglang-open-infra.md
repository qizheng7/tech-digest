## Summary

RadixArk, the company commercializing SGLang (the open-source inference engine powering trillions of tokens daily at Google, Microsoft, xAI, and others), launched publicly with $100M in seed funding at a $400M valuation. Led by Accel and Spark Capital, with participation from NVIDIA, AMD, and an all-star roster of AI founders, the company is built around two open-source systems: SGLang for inference and Miles for reinforcement learning and post-training. The mission is to make production-grade frontier AI infrastructure — scheduling, KV-cache management, rollout systems — openly accessible rather than forcing every team to rebuild from scratch.

## Key Highlights

- $100M seed at $400M valuation, led by Accel and Spark Capital; NVIDIA (NVentures) and AMD also invested
- Angel investors include John Schulman (OpenAI co-founder), Soumith Chintala (PyTorch creator/CTO Thinking Machines), Thomas Wolf (Hugging Face co-founder), Igor Babuschkin (xAI co-founder), and Lip-Bu Tan (Intel CEO)
- SGLang already powers trillions of tokens/day for Google, Microsoft, Oracle, xAI, LinkedIn, and others
- Built on two OSS foundations: SGLang (inference) and Miles (RL/post-training)
- Founded by Ying Sheng and Banghua Zhu, AI infra veterans from xAI and NVIDIA
- Goals: accelerate support for emerging model architectures, frontier hardware, and large-scale inference + training infra

## Why It Matters

RadixArk's launch signals that the SGLang ecosystem has reached critical mass — the NVIDIA/AMD co-investment alongside top AI founders validates open inference infrastructure as the key unseized leverage point in the AI stack.
