## Summary

Anthropic announced the formation of a new enterprise AI services firm co-founded with private equity giants Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, and Goldman Sachs, with further backing from General Atlantic, Leonard Green, Apollo Global Management, GIC, and Sequoia Capital. The firm will embed engineering teams—including Anthropic Applied AI staff—directly at client organizations to identify high-impact use cases, build custom Claude-powered solutions, and provide ongoing support. The target market is mid-sized companies that lack the in-house resources to implement enterprise AI on their own. As Anthropic CFO Krishna Rao put it: "Enterprise demand for Claude is significantly outpacing any single delivery model."

## Key Highlights

- Partners in the new firm: Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, Goldman Sachs; additional consortium: General Atlantic, Leonard Green, Apollo, GIC, Sequoia
- Teams will embed directly at client sites to build and deploy Claude-powered workflows
- Target segment: mid-sized organizations without in-house AI implementation capacity
- Joins Accenture, Deloitte, and PwC in Anthropic's Claude Partner Network
- Illustrative use case: helping clinicians reduce administrative documentation and coding burden
- Anthropic's first explicit push into professional services infrastructure at scale

## Why It Matters

Anthropic is building a PE-backed professional services arm to capture mid-market AI adoption that traditional consulting firms—optimized for large enterprise—are too slow or expensive to serve.
