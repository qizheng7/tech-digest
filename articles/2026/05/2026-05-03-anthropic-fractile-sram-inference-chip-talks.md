## Summary

Anthropic is in early-stage talks to procure AI inference chips from Fractile, a UK startup founded by Oxford PhD Walter Goodwin that co-locates SRAM memory directly with compute on the same die, eliminating the off-chip DRAM data movement bottleneck that limits throughput on conventional GPU inference. The deal, first reported by The Information, would make Fractile Anthropic's fourth chip supplier alongside Nvidia, Google, and Amazon. Fractile's chips are not expected to reach commercial readiness until ~2027, and no agreement has been finalized.

## Key Highlights

- Fractile's architecture: SRAM co-located with compute on-die, removing the GPU-to-DRAM memory bandwidth bottleneck that is a primary constraint for large-model inference
- Founded 2022 by Walter Goodwin (Oxford PhD); based in London
- Anthropic's compute context: annualized revenue ~$30B as of April 2026 (up from ~$9B at end of 2025), with 1,000+ enterprise customers each spending $1M+ annually straining inference supply
- Would add Fractile as a 4th chip supplier alongside existing Nvidia, Google (TPU), and Amazon (Trainium) relationships
- Chip commercial readiness target: ~2027 — aligns with same window as Anthropic's Google-Broadcom TPU partnership
- Discussions are preliminary; no terms or timeline finalized

## Why It Matters

Anthropic is aggressively diversifying its chip supply stack as inference demand outpaces what any single hyperscaler can provide, and SRAM-based inference architectures — if they scale — could materially change the economics of serving frontier models.
