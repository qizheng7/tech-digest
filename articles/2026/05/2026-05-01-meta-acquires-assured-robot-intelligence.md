## Summary

Meta acquired Assured Robot Intelligence (ARI), a startup building foundation models for humanoid robots to understand and adapt to human behavior in complex environments, with the team joining Meta Superintelligence Labs. Founders Xiaolong Wang (former Nvidia researcher, UCSD professor) and Lerrel Pinto (NYU, previously co-founded Fauna Robotics acquired by Amazon) will lead work on whole-body humanoid control and robot self-learning. Financial terms were not disclosed; ARI had raised seed funding from AIX Ventures.

## Key Highlights

- ARI's mission: foundation models enabling robots to perform physical tasks — household chores, dynamic environment navigation, human behavior prediction
- Founders: Xiaolong Wang (Nvidia research, UCSD, multiple best-paper awards) and Lerrel Pinto (NYU; prior company Fauna Robotics acquired by Amazon)
- Team joins Meta Superintelligence Labs research division — Meta's frontier AI research org
- Meta's strategy: build the "Android/Qualcomm" platform for humanoid robotics — infrastructure for the broader industry
- Analysts note physical robot training could be critical path for AGI via embodied experience
- Meta's ongoing humanoid interest: earlier discussions of consumer-facing robots; this is the explicit research arm

## Why It Matters

Meta is positioning Superintelligence Labs as the central hub for physical AI, signaling that the race for humanoid robotics foundation models has moved from speculation to strategic acqui-hires among the hyperscalers.
