## Summary

Nebius (NASDAQ: NBIS) announced an agreement to acquire Eigen AI, an inference and model optimization startup founded by MIT HAN Lab alumni, for approximately $643M ($98M cash + 3.8M Nebius shares). Eigen AI's optimization layers — including Activation-aware Weight Quantization (AWQ) and sparse attention techniques — will be integrated directly into Nebius Token Factory, the company's enterprise-grade managed inference platform for open-source models. The team will establish a new Bay Area engineering hub post-close.

## Key Highlights

- Deal: ~$643M total ($98M cash + 3.8M Nebius shares); team joins Nebius and sets up Bay Area hub
- Eigen AI founders: Ryan Hanrui Wang (Sparse Attention — most-cited HPCA paper since 2020), Wei-Chen Wang (AWQ — MLSys 2024 Best Paper), Di Jin (MIT CSAIL PhD, post-training and model alignment)
- Eigen AI's tech integrates directly into Nebius Token Factory: enterprise autoscaling endpoints + fine-tuning pipelines across major open-source models
- Token Factory becomes a "frontier managed inference platform" with hardware-efficiency optimizations at the kernel level
- Nebius also gains US engineering presence and academic research network through the acquisition

## Why It Matters

The deal consolidates two layers of the open-source inference stack — cloud infrastructure (Nebius) and kernel-level model optimization (Eigen AI) — and signals that inference platforms are competing on hardware efficiency as much as API reliability.
