## Summary

Rogo, an agentic AI platform built for financial services, raised $160M in Series D funding led by Kleiner Perkins, with participation from Sequoia, Thrive Capital, Khosla Ventures, and J.P. Morgan Growth Equity Partners. The round brings total funding to over $300M. Rogo's platform — anchored by its AI agent Felix — serves 35,000+ professionals at 250+ financial institutions including Rothschild, Jefferies, Lazard, and Moelis. Felix handles multi-step finance tasks autonomously: deal screening, CIM generation, buyer outreach, and data room diligence.

## Key Highlights

- **$160M Series D** led by Kleiner Perkins; Sequoia, Thrive Capital, Khosla, J.P. Morgan Growth Equity participating; total funding >$300M
- **35,000+ users** at 250+ institutions including top-tier investment banks and PE firms
- **Felix (AI agent):** handles full deal workflows autonomously — screening, CIM creation, buyer outreach, data room diligence — without human intervention on each step
- **Purpose-built financial reasoning models** combined with deep integrations into internal/external data sources
- **Use of funds:** global expansion, deeper partnerships with financial institutions, scaling Felix

## Why It Matters

Rogo's raise signals that financial services is becoming one of the highest-conviction verticals for agentic AI — purpose-built vertical agents with deep domain data integrations and regulatory sensitivity are commanding premium valuations, and Kleiner/Sequoia backing gives Rogo the capital to dominate before the big horizontal platforms move in.
