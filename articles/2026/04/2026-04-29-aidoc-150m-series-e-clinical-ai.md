## Summary

Aidoc, a clinical AI company focused on radiology and imaging, raised $150M in Series E funding led by Goldman Sachs Alternatives, with participation from General Catalyst, SoftBank Investment Advisors, and NVIDIA's NVentures. The round brings total funding to over $500M and positions the company for a potential IPO. Aidoc's platform analyzes over 60 million patient cases annually across nearly 2,000 hospitals, and its CARE foundation model recently received a landmark FDA clearance for a comprehensive imaging triage system.

## Key Highlights

- **$150M Series E** led by Growth Equity at Goldman Sachs Alternatives; total funding now >$500M; Axios reports IPO conversations are ongoing
- **Deployed at ~2,000 hospitals**, analyzing 60M+ patient cases per year across radiology and clinical imaging workflows
- **CARE foundation model** — first FDA-cleared comprehensive foundation model-based triage system for clinical imaging; funds will expand into new clinical indications
- **New capabilities:** automated imaging draft report creation for end-to-end radiology workflows
- **aiOS platform** lets hospitals consolidate standalone AI tools under a centralized governance/management layer

## Why It Matters

Aidoc is one of the first clinical AI companies to achieve both scale (2,000 hospitals) and regulatory legitimacy (FDA-cleared foundation model), suggesting the healthcare vertical is entering a consolidation phase where enterprise AI platforms with compliance depth will outcompete point solutions.
