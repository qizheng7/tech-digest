## Summary

On April 29, 2026, Alphabet, Microsoft, Meta, and Amazon all reported Q1 2026 earnings that validated massive AI infrastructure spending, with combined 2026 capex commitments approaching $650B. Google Cloud crossed $20B in quarterly revenue for the first time, AWS posted its fastest growth in 15 quarters at 28%, and Microsoft's AI business reached a $37B annualized run rate — all beating analyst expectations. Meta's stock dipped 7% after raising its full-year capex guidance to $125-145B for "superintelligence" research, even as its core business grew 33%.

## Key Highlights

- **Alphabet**: Revenue $109.9B (+20% YoY, highest growth since 2022); net income $62.58B (+81%); Google Cloud +63% YoY with $460B backlog; CEO Pichai: "cloud revenue would have been higher if we could meet the demand"
- **Microsoft**: Revenue $82.9B (+18%); AI business at $37B annualized run rate (+123% YoY); cloud revenue $54.5B (+29%)
- **Meta**: Revenue $56.3B (+33%, fastest since 2021); net income $26.8B (+61%); stock fell ~7% after-hours on raised capex to $125-145B for 2026
- **Amazon**: Revenue $133.9B; AWS revenue $37.5B (+28%, fastest growth in 15 quarters); record AWS operating margins
- **Aggregate signal**: All four companies beat revenue and earnings estimates; combined $650B+ in 2026 AI infrastructure commitments

## Why It Matters

The earnings collectively prove AI infrastructure investment is generating measurable cloud revenue acceleration, but Meta's stock reaction shows investors are increasingly scrutinizing whether marginal capex increases will translate into proportional returns — a tension that will define the next phase of the AI buildout.
