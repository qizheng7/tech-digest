## Summary

Latent.Space hosted Alex Lupsasca (physicist, New Horizons in Fundamental Physics Breakthrough Prize 2024, now at OpenAI) for a 92-minute episode on how GPT-5.x derived genuinely new results in theoretical physics. The episode covers two breakthroughs: GPT-5.2 solving a long-stalled problem in gluon tree amplitudes (reproducing Lupsasca's best paper in 11 minutes, then finding a proof technique the authors didn't know), and a subsequent "vibe physics" run where GPT generated 110 pages of novel graviton amplitude results in under three days. The broader point: AI capability advances are largely invisible to users who measure it by email quality, but dramatic at the scientific frontier.

## Key Highlights

- GPT-5.2 reproduced Lupsasca's hardest paper (which had taken a long time to write) in 11 minutes, after training cutoff — the model wasn't memorizing it
- The model found a "half-collinear regime" limiting case that simplified a 32-term expression to a few terms on one line — a technique the authors didn't know to use
- "Vibe physics" session: GPT autonomously generated 110 pages of new graviton amplitude physics in under a day; the team spent three weeks verifying it
- The Jagged Frontier observation: GPT lukewarmly received by people testing email quality, but "capabilities were really taking off" at the science frontier
- Lupsasca's framing: with AI generating results "as fast as we can conceive and validate them," a single theorist's scope of achievable work has "gotten a lot, lot bigger"

## Why It Matters

Vibe Physics is to theoretical physics what Vibe Coding is to software — but with a key difference: new physics means genuinely extending the frontier of human knowledge, not just recombining existing patterns.
