## Summary

Latent.Space's podcast episode with Applied Intuition co-founders Qasar Younis and Peter Ludwig covers how they built a $15B physical AI company from YC-era autonomy tooling. Applied Intuition provides simulation, data, and software tools for companies building autonomous systems — vehicles, robots, and industrial machines. The episode covers the company's trajectory, the physical AI market's evolution, and what it takes to productionize AI for real-world kinetic systems.

## Key Highlights

- Applied Intuition valued at $15B — one of the most valuable AI infrastructure companies in the physical AI space
- Founded from YC-era roots; grew by targeting the hard problem of simulation and testing for autonomous systems
- Products serve automotive OEMs, defense, robotics, and industrial automation customers
- Physical AI framed as distinct from software AI: different data loops, safety requirements, and iteration cycles
- Podcast covers market dynamics, enterprise go-to-market, and the gap between research demos and production deployment
- Published April 27, 2026 by Latent.Space (swyx & alessio)

## Why It Matters

Applied Intuition's $15B scale shows physical AI infrastructure is a distinct and massive market — and that the tooling layer for autonomous systems mirrors the SaaS tooling revolution for software, with simulation/data as the critical moat.
