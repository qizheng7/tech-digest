## Summary

Claire Vo's hands-on GPT-5.5 review for Lenny's "How I AI" podcast documents two real-world tests: a 6-hour autonomous data migration (2M rows, zero follow-up prompts) and cracking a proprietary Bluetooth speaker protocol that Claude Code, GPT-4, and other models couldn't solve. Her conclusion: GPT-5.5 is transformatively useful for complex technical problems but overkill for everyday tasks, and the ROI over human engineering time is clear for hard problems.

## Key Highlights

- 6-hour autonomous migration: 2M rows of unstructured data, zero steering, only one approval request; "I trust you, figure it out" prompt unlocked the behavior
- Bluetooth hack: 6 months of failed attempts with other models solved by GPT-5.5 using packet sniffers + Chinese documentation; now controls speaker from terminal via Codex notification hooks
- GPT-5.5 Pro: $30/M input, $180/M output — cheaper than human engineering time for hard problems
- Most ChatGPT users won't find everyday use cases; model spent 17 minutes thinking about a simple subtraction app
- "/personality" slash command in Codex fixes default "baked potato" personality
- Verdict: real value unlocks only with genuinely hard, complex technical problems

## Why It Matters

The 6-hour autonomous task is the clearest public demonstration of GPT-5.5's step-change in agentic capability — showing that long-running unattended workflows are no longer aspirational but available today for practitioners with the right problem.
